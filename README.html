Heart Failure Risk and Dietary Sodium & Protein Intake

Laboratory Project in Health Data Science — HEADS Doctoral Programme, FMUP

Research Question

What is the association between estimated habitual sodium and protein intake and the prevalence of self-reported heart failure in the US adult population, adjusting for socioeconomic determinants and metabolic comorbidities?

The project investigates whether energy-adjusted dietary sodium and protein intake are independently associated with heart failure (HF) prevalence, after controlling for age, sex, income, education, BMI, diabetes, systolic blood pressure, and antihypertensive/diuretic medication use.

Methods Summary

Energy Adjustment — Willett’s Residual Method

Sodium and protein intakes are adjusted for total energy intake to separate the effect of dietary composition from total food volume. Survey-weighted linear regression (svyglm) of each nutrient on total energy (DR1TKCAL) is fitted, and residuals are extracted and re-centred at the weighted population mean.

Statistical Model

A survey-weighted quasi-binomial logistic regression (svyglm, quasibinomial family) is fitted using the survey package, incorporating PSUs, strata, and adjusted sample weights. The quasi-binomial family is appropriate for complex survey binary outcomes as it handles overdispersion relative to standard binomial.

Multi-Cycle Weight Adjustment

Four 2-year NHANES cycles are pooled (2011–2018). Per CDC guidelines for multi-cycle pooling, WTMEC2YR is divided by 4 to produce Weight_MEC8, which represents the 8-year reference population.

Complete Case Analysis

Participants with missing values in any model covariate are excluded prior to survey design declaration. The number and proportion excluded are reported transparently in Section 2.1 of the report.

How to Reproduce

Requirements

  • R ≥ 4.2.0
  • Internet access (for the data_import chunk only — CDC NHANES API)

R Packages

install.packages(c(
  "haven", "nhanesA", "readr", "dplyr", "tidyr", "purrr",
  "survey", "stringr", "tableone", "knitr", "kableExtra",
  "ggplot2", "pROC", "car", "shiny"
))

Step-by-step

Step 1 — Generate the cleaned dataset (only needed once)

Open final_draft_report.Rmd and set the data_import chunk option to eval=TRUE, then run that chunk. This will download data from the CDC NHANES API and write the following files to data/: - clean_dataset.rds - target_population.rds

Reset eval=FALSE after the files are saved.

Step 2 — Render the full report

rmarkdown::render("final_draft_report.Rmd")

This will produce final_draft_report.pdf and write data/final_model.rds and data/dashboard_matrix.rds.

Step 3 — Launch the Shiny dashboard

shiny::runApp("app.R")

The dashboard reads data/final_model.rds and data/dashboard_matrix.rds — these must exist before launching (generated in Step 2).

Key Results

Results are fully described in final_draft_report.pdf. In summary:

  • Survey-weighted logistic regression models the adjusted association between energy-normalised sodium/protein intake and HF prevalence across the representative US adult population.
  • Odds Ratios with 95% confidence intervals are reported for all predictors.
  • Model discrimination is assessed via AUC (ROC curve).
  • Multicollinearity is evaluated using Generalised Variance Inflation Factors (GVIF).
  • A predicted risk curve across the P5–P95 range of Willett-adjusted sodium intake is provided for a reference patient profile.

Limitations

  1. Cross-sectional design — causality cannot be inferred; reverse causation is plausible (HF patients may modify their diet after diagnosis).
  2. Self-reported outcome — HF diagnosis relies on participant self-report (MCQ160B); no biomarker or imaging confirmation is available in NHANES.
  3. Single 24-hour dietary recall — may not reflect habitual intake; Willett’s method partially mitigates within-person variability.
  4. Complete case analysis — missingness correlates with socioeconomic status; the analytic sample may over-represent higher-income adults.
  5. Protein origin classification — based on the first digit of USDA FNDDS food codes, which is a coarse approximation for mixed or processed foods.
  6. Medication variable scope — antihypertensive/diuretic flag is based on active ingredient name matching; trade names not in the lookup list may be missed.

LLM Usage

All interactions with Large Language Models during this project are documented in prompts.md, including prompts used, model responses, and critical commentary by the group.
